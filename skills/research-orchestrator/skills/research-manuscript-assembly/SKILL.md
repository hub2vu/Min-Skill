# Research Manuscript Assembly

Use this skill when the user is working toward a paper, article, note, or other scholarly write-up.

## Operating Rules

- keep the topic fixed unless the user changes it
- judge whether the current evidence honestly supports the requested form
- if readiness is insufficient, stay on the same topic and provide only the narrower supported form, such as a memo, protocol, evidence map, or proof note
- once writing readiness is honestly met, treat manuscript work as the default active mode unless regression is required
- if the evidence honestly supports the requested form, build the manuscript cumulatively across turns rather than forcing one-turn completion
- accumulate bounded sections or subsections and preserve those cumulative writing artifacts in the ZIP
- when public archive delivery is in scope, keep those section artifacts in the public research archive rather than in the operating bundle
- when a new section opens, preserve why that section is opening now and which source turns support it
- do not treat the first writing-entry turn as a fully complete paper
- do not jump to an integrated manuscript unless preserved section work, claim coverage, and integration readiness honestly exist
- do not jump to a fully connected manuscript unless the user explicitly asks for completion or prior accumulated-section artifacts already exist and make full completion honestly supportable
- when manuscript-closing work starts, move bibliography, source log, and manuscript references onto one canonical citation ledger
- keep competing explanations and the judgment rule visible in the manuscript when the genre is interpretive review
- keep the manuscript clear enough that direct evidence, synthesis, and remaining uncertainty are distinguishable
- keep process jargon out of manuscript prose
- keep missing accountability or disclosure fields explicit instead of inventing them
- match claim width to design and evidence
- if writing exposes an unsupported gap, regress to the earlier research stage instead of writing through the gap
- when a final manuscript bundle is honestly ready and the runtime supports it, co-deliver Markdown, an editable manuscript artifact when appropriate, and PDF; otherwise state the exact format boundary

## Completion Rule

When the paper is actually complete and the user asks for the manuscript or for completion, return the whole connected paper in one answer unless the user asks for only a section or delta. That final connected manuscript should be assembled from the cumulative writing work preserved across prior turns, not improvised as a disconnected one-shot replacement.

## Discussion Rule

Any long memo or manuscript should still close with a short discussion, conclusion, or implications section before references when the genre allows it.
