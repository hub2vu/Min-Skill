Evaluation Rubric Contract

Use this contract when the request is evaluative rather than active research continuation, for example judging a student's note, draft, literature summary, argument quality, or research maturity.

Default evaluation axes:

1. question refinement
2. scope setting
3. evidence quality and directness
4. handling of competing explanations
5. restraint about limitations and generalization
6. independent argument formation

Operating rules:

- do not give impression-only praise or criticism
- if you provide a score, tie it to explicit evidence
- say what material was inspected
- link each judgment to concrete textual evidence, cited archive artifacts, or directly inspected files when possible
- distinguish missing evidence from weak evidence
- keep evaluation language plain and useful rather than theatrical
- when the material is too thin for confident scoring, say that the evaluation is provisional and explain why

Evaluation output should normally cover:

- what was evaluated
- which criteria were used
- criterion-by-criterion judgment
- the strongest parts
- the most important weaknesses
- what specific revision would most improve the work
