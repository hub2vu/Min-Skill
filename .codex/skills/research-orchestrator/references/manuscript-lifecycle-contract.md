Manuscript Lifecycle Contract

Writing readiness is a gate, not a mood.

Do not enter section drafting until all of the following are honestly present:

- a stabilized research question and scope
- defined core concepts and evidence standards
- a mapped prior-art landscape
- evidence collection with quality appraisal
- competing explanation comparison
- an explicit argument direction and limitations note
- a preserved claim-evidence map
- at least one major open gap resolved enough to support bounded prose
- full connected-manuscript readiness still not honestly met

If those conditions are not met, allow only narrower outputs such as:

- evidence memo
- literature map
- concept note
- protocol
- outline
- section stub

Bounded section accumulation belongs in the public research archive when a public archive is in scope. Keep section files topic-centered and free of operating-bundle narration.

Once writing readiness is met, use this lifecycle:

1. section drafting and accumulation
   - accumulate bounded sections
   - each newly opened section should preserve provenance fields that explain why the section opened now and which source turns support it
   - do not complete the whole paper on the first writing turn
2. integration and revision
   - integrate preserved sections
   - align abstract, introduction, approach or methods, analysis or results, discussion, limitations, and conclusion as appropriate for the genre
   - for literature-based interpretive reviews, make competing explanations and the judgment rule visible in the manuscript as a dedicated subsection or clearly separated paragraph group rather than leaving them only in notes
   - keep the prose clear enough that a reader can distinguish direct evidence, cross-source synthesis, and remaining uncertainty
3. final manuscript completion readiness
   - perform completion checks
   - assemble the full connected manuscript only when explicit completion is requested or the workflow is honestly ready

Integration requires more than turn count. At minimum it needs:

- preserved section files from more than one writing turn
- evidence-backed section content rather than empty stubs
- claim coverage that matches the integrated draft
- a consistency or coherence audit
- a canonical citation ledger with manuscript-used sources resolved, including author-list integrity and locator fields where the source family uses them
- bibliography, source log, and manuscript references aligned to that ledger
- citation-style consistency across the connected manuscript
- final-manuscript validator checks honestly met

If drafting exposes unsupported gaps, unstable definitions, weak evidence linkage, or unresolved contradictions, regress to the earliest honest research stage instead of writing through the problem.

For bounded section drafting, preserve explicit provenance when files are writable. A section record should normally state:

- `Created_at`
- `Updated_at`
- `Timezone`
- `Turn_id`
- `Section_status`
- `Opened_because`
- `Source_turns`

In the public body, say plainly when bounded section drafting is now justified while full-manuscript completion is still not ready.

When a final manuscript bundle is honestly ready and the runtime supports file generation, normally deliver:

- the connected manuscript in Markdown
- an editable manuscript artifact when appropriate
- PDF

If a required format cannot be produced, state the exact boundary rather than implying full delivery.
