# Research Evidence Loop

Use this skill when fresher or broader evidence could materially improve correctness, completeness, or justified confidence.

## When To Search

Search when:

- the fact may be stale
- source precision matters
- package or API behavior may have changed
- repository, issue, release, or documentation state matters
- standards, papers, or ecosystem comparisons are central
- your confidence is not high enough to answer safely from inspected local evidence alone

## Operating Loop

1. anchor relative dates to the runtime's current date or time when available
2. form a short search strategy before the first query
3. prefer official documentation, source repositories, standards bodies, registries, canonical paper pages, and other high-trust sources
4. cross-check important claims across more than one source family when practical
5. preserve explicit rationale artifacts for search strategy, inclusion logic, conflict handling, and sufficiency or insufficiency judgments when durable research artifacts are being updated
6. when manuscript-closing work is active, verify citation identity against direct originals or official index records before treating a source as canonically closed
7. continue until freshness, coverage, contradiction checks, and justified confidence are strong enough
8. present conclusions with clear source attribution

## Tool Rules

- use the runtime web tool as the default search path when available
- use python or another calculation tool for concrete counts, comparisons, or consistency checks when that strengthens the answer
- when a tool or source family is unavailable, state that exact boundary and proceed with verified facts, clearly marked assumptions, and open questions

## Output Rules

- separate verified fact from inference
- do not present memory as inspected evidence
- do not let short state summaries replace explicit research rationale when the workflow is preserving durable research artifacts
- do not treat a few bullets or a short run-state note as enough rationale; explain what was learned, what conflicts mattered, why sufficiency is or is not met, how the argument changed, and what next research or writing move is unlocked
- when the work matures, distinguish what is now stable, what narrowed on the current turn, what newly opened, and what remains not ready instead of flattening everything into completion language
- preserve generative artifacts when the workflow is moving beyond literature summary, especially competing explanations, conceptual refinement, and claim-evidence mapping
- keep claim width narrower than the evidence can honestly support
- when uncertainty remains material, say so and keep the next action narrow and explicit
